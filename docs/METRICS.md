# North Star Metrics

## 1. Audit Completion Rate
- **Definition**: Percentage of users who land on the `/audit` page and successfully submit the form.
- **Target**: >60%
- **Why**: The form is the top of the funnel. If this is low, the form is too complex or we aren't establishing enough trust on the landing page.

## 2. Lead Capture Conversion Rate
- **Definition**: Percentage of users who see their savings estimate and submit their email to get the full report.
- **Target**: >30% (highly dependent on the savings number shown).
- **Why**: This is the core engine of the business. Free audits mean nothing if we can't capture the lead for consulting/brokerage.

## 3. Viral Coefficient (k-factor)
- **Definition**: Number of new users generated by each existing user sharing their report URL.
- **Target**: >0.2
- **Why**: A high k-factor drastically lowers our CAC. The report must be visually stunning to encourage sharing on LinkedIn/Twitter.

## 4. Average Identified Savings
- **Definition**: The average monthly savings identified per audit.
- **Why**: This tells us if we are targeting the right size of companies. If it's consistently under $50/mo, we are hitting freelancers instead of our target B2B demographic.
